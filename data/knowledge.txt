RSI (Relative Strength Index) measures momentum on a scale of 0-100. Above 70 is overbought, below 30 is oversold.
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) shows trend direction and momentum. A bullish crossover occurs when MACD crosses above the signal line.
Moving averages smooth price data. The 50-day and 200-day MAs are key levels. A golden cross (50 crosses above 200) is bullish. A death cross is bearish.
Volume confirms price moves. High volume on a breakout is bullish. Low volume on a rally is suspicious.
Support is a price level where buying pressure exceeds selling. Resistance is where selling exceeds buying.
Bollinger Bands measure volatility. Price touching the upper band suggests overbought. Lower band suggests oversold.
The P/E ratio compares stock price to earnings. High P/E means the market expects high growth. Low P/E may indicate undervaluation.
EPS measures company profitability. Growing EPS quarter over quarter is bullish.
Revenue growth is a key fundamental metric. Consistent double-digit growth is considered strong for tech companies.
Debt to equity ratio measures financial leverage. High D/E can indicate risk especially in rising rate environments.
Free cash flow is cash a company generates after capital expenditures. Strong FCF allows buybacks, dividends, and acquisitions.
Market cap classifies company size. Large cap over 10B, mid cap 2-10B, small cap 300M-2B.
Beta measures volatility relative to the market. Beta above 1 means more volatile than the market.
Diversification reduces portfolio risk by spreading across sectors and asset classes.
Dollar cost averaging means investing fixed amounts regularly regardless of price.
A bull market is a sustained rise of 20% or more. A bear market is a sustained decline of 20% or more.
Options give the right but not obligation to buy or sell at a specific price. Calls are bullish, puts are bearish.
Short selling involves borrowing shares and selling them hoping to buy back cheaper.
Technical analysis uses price charts and indicators to predict future price movements.
Fundamental analysis evaluates a company's financial health and intrinsic value.
Candlestick patterns like doji, hammer, and engulfing patterns signal potential reversals.
The Fibonacci retracement levels 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8% are key support and resistance zones.
Average True Range (ATR) measures market volatility. High ATR means larger price swings.
The VIX index measures market fear. Above 30 signals high fear. Below 20 signals complacency.
Stop loss orders automatically sell a position when it hits a predetermined price to limit losses.

SWING TRADING - MULTI-MONTH MOVES (3 months to 2 years)
Swing trading targets large directional moves over weeks to months, not day-to-day noise.
The best swing trades combine a strong fundamental catalyst with a technical setup - the catalyst drives the move, the technical setup gives you the entry.
A stage 2 breakout occurs when a stock breaks above a long base on high volume after a period of accumulation. This is the highest probability swing trade setup.
Relative strength is the most important swing trading indicator. Stocks that hold up during market selloffs and break out first during recoveries tend to make the biggest moves.
Cup and handle patterns on weekly charts are among the most reliable swing trade setups for 3-12 month moves.
The weekly chart is the primary timeframe for swing trades. Daily charts are used for entry timing only.

HOT SECTORS AND CATALYSTS
AI infrastructure is the dominant theme - companies providing compute (NVIDIA, AMD), cloud (AWS, Azure, GCP), and data infrastructure are direct beneficiaries.
Semiconductor equipment is a bottleneck for AI expansion. ASML, Applied Materials, and Lam Research control critical steps in chip manufacturing.
Power and energy infrastructure is a critical bottleneck for AI data centers. Utilities, natural gas, and nuclear energy companies benefit from surging data center power demand.
Cybersecurity spending is accelerating as AI expands attack surfaces. Zero trust architecture companies see growing enterprise demand.
GLP-1 weight loss drugs are creating ripple effects - companies in drug delivery, medical devices, and food are all impacted.
Defense and aerospace benefit from rising geopolitical tensions and government spending cycles.
Reshoring of manufacturing creates multi-year tailwinds for industrial automation, robotics, and domestic manufacturing.
Healthcare AI is early stage but growing - companies applying AI to drug discovery, diagnostics, and clinical workflow automation.
Space economy is expanding - satellite internet, launch services, and space-based data collection are multi-year growth themes.

SECTOR BOTTLENECKS
AI compute bottleneck: NVIDIA H100/H200 GPUs have 12+ month lead times.
Power grid bottleneck: US power grid cannot support projected data center growth. Utilities, transformer manufacturers, and nuclear energy companies benefit.
Semiconductor advanced packaging bottleneck: CoWoS packaging from TSMC is constrained.
Cooling technology bottleneck: Traditional air cooling cannot handle next-gen GPU heat density. Liquid cooling companies are beneficiaries.
Rare earth minerals bottleneck: EV batteries and defense systems require rare earth elements. Companies with domestic rare earth supply chains have strategic value.

SIGNALS FOR BIG MOVES
Government contract wins are major catalysts for defense, IT services, and cloud companies.
Hyperscaler capex announcements directly signal demand for AI infrastructure suppliers.
Drug approval catalysts (FDA PDUFA dates) create binary events for biotech stocks.
Partnership announcements between large enterprises and AI companies validate technology.
Index inclusion (S&P 500 or Russell 2000 additions) forces passive fund buying.
Share buyback announcements signal management confidence and reduce float.
Insider buying clusters - when multiple executives buy shares simultaneously - is one of the strongest bullish signals.
Earnings estimate revisions upward by Wall Street analysts precede institutional buying.

SWING TRADE SETUP CHECKLIST
Strong relative strength vs S&P 500 over last 3-6 months.
Consolidating near 52-week highs, not near lows.
Identifiable fundamental catalyst - new product, contract win, sector tailwind.
Increasing institutional ownership quarter over quarter.
Revenue acceleration - growth rate increasing, not just growing.
Tight price action (low ATR) in the base before breakout.
Volume dry up during consolidation followed by volume expansion on breakout.
Short interest above 10% - potential for short squeeze amplifying the move.
Sector in favor with market - swimming with the tide not against it.
Clear price target based on measured move from the base pattern.

NEWS AND SENTIMENT SIGNALS
Earnings surprises of 20%+ with raised guidance are the most reliable catalyst for sustained multi-month moves.
Management guidance language matters - words like accelerating, unprecedented demand, supply constrained signal a stock about to move.
Short squeeze conditions: high short interest plus positive catalyst equals explosive upside.
Analyst upgrades with significant price target increases attract institutional attention.
Viral product launches or consumer adoption data can reprice a stock's growth multiple overnight.
Regulatory approvals remove binary risk and unlock institutional buying.
Strategic acquisitions at premiums reveal sector valuations.
